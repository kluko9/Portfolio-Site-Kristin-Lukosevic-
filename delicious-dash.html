Delicious Dash

About

When creating Delicious Dash a lot of research had to be done to see exactly what users need in a recipe app. The idea for Delicious Dash was targeted to help users who are on the go and do not have a lot of time when it comes to their meals. The app offers a variety of food groups and filtering options to find exactly what users need for their meal. The app also incorporates a helpful feature to substitute an ingredient limiting user frustration when it comes to cooking a recipe.

The Who, When, Why, and Whats of Delicious Dash

Who

Who will use this app?
Delicious Dash users are people who are eating by themselves or preparing for others. Users are people who are on the go with minimal time.

When

When will the product be used?
In the kitchen, in the grocery store, or any kind of food gathering.

Why

Why does the user need this product?
The user needs this product as it focuses on quick recipes for whoever providing for. Whether that's one person or multiple people.

What

What major goals and tasks will the users want/need?

Searching for recipes with specific filters

  • Find quick and delicious recipes
  • Adjust time for a recipe such as less than 30 mins, less than one hour
  • Filtering how many people they will be cooking for
  • Saving and tweaking recipes based on their experience with cooking
  • Substituting ingredients when users do not have everything on hand

Problem

The main problem with recipe apps is that users will search for something and the recipe that shows up will not be at all what they searched for.

User Interviews

After talking with users a few common problems came about. Out of the 3 users I interviewed the problems included.

  • Users want a recipe app that will allow minimal cooking time as they have busy schedules.
  • Users would like the ability to filter how many people they are cooking for and have recipes that can be adjusted based off of that.
  • Users would like the option to substitute an ingredient if they are missing something.
  • Users want recipes that are what they are searching for based of dietary needs or restrictions.

Solutions

To fix the common problems Delicious Dash the following feature were implemented

  • Filtering options for
    • Time to cook
    • Amount of people cooking for
    • Dietary needs and or restrictions
  • Option to substitue a missing ingredient for a recipe
  • Option to save recipe and adjust based off of cooking taste

Summary

Throughout this design process, I have learned a lot as far as what is expected and what users need. The initial challenges were focusing on what my app will offer that will make it stick out from the crowd. Through user research, I have determined that having a “substitute ingredient” section would really help users in almost all searches. The challenges throughout this project were creating a combination of dark background colors with bright images and lettering. I wanted to ensure that the lettering and images would not be hard on the eyes. If I could go back and recreate this app I would probably focus my design on a specific type of food like healthy food or desserts. The specific topic would have helped me really focus my ideas on one type of product vs all of them.
